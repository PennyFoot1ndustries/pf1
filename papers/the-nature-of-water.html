The Nature of Water

In cases of emergency related to drought and water shortages we find the most peculiar relationship between humanity and it most precious resource. Without water in it's specific hydro-genetics, life could not have nor could continue. The polarity of H20 makes it ideal to be a miracle solvent given the right conditions. It appears that its scarcity can be related mostly to human conflict save for a drought (which arguably can be controlled by human meddling). The thermodynamics of our study would say 100% of the water the Earth began with eons ago has been conserved over that same time period. The nature of our planet gives rise to a stratosphere and further reaching environmental conditions that lend the border of our planet's sphere keeps all the water within the chaos of "weather". If I drink water here on the land mass of North Carolina, the water is for a time unavailable along the Yellow River in mainland China. But eventually my body will digest it, it will process it along with other ingredients to help me survive and it will pass as mostly sweat and urine. It will then run down the runoff trails into the water table and balance with weather systems all around the world. Not instantly. But over time, all the water that existed on the planet to begin with is maintained at similar levels. Now lets say I have a need for water that isnt drinking. And I gorge my need for water on the surplus I see in my immediate area. Do I deprive anyone of anything? At a certain point, I believe it can be synonymous with soft flatulence over a distance of negligible accuracy to the mile. If we have shortages of water they are most likely due to human decision making and actions. Some of the most important decisions we need to make are related to water, its availabilty, and its quality.
