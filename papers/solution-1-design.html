Solution 1 Design

to design with solution 1 design is to examine the nature of design within a scalar range at every instance of deliberation regarding the deliverables or intent of the design. for instance, you not only have the finished product witin a design. you also have design intent. i regularly design with consideration being weighed for the embodied energy, material cost and use, the strength to weight ratio, the cost for production compared to cost of prototype, among others. if the needs of a design call for the finished product to complete a task, you can design for that task to begin with and then apply non client focused design techniques to reap a benefit. an example of this is when business designs planned obselescence. in order to recoup capital losses they can engineer a market of return customers from the fsilure of the last part if its breakage doesnt negate their trust. but the full spectrum of a 0-1 allows for other design tactics to impart a successful design. an example of how to picture "solution 1" criteria could be the following: a home has needs for a fixing of the dog eating the trash. a good solution 1 design would draw the system as the client such that the system is understood by the solution; not forced to obey its parameters. does the dog need to be a part of the house? does the house need to be remodeled and can be ferried in a new waste disposal system? do the people even need the home? these are extremes but as you may see, they offer a large array of possibilities contrary to yelling at the dog, picking up after them, and doing nothing different. the ladder would be ~0.15 design. a new home with the dog on vacation to the dog park could be a 0.97 design. the scale?
